What a Soft Wash Is

High pressure is great for concrete and wrong for houses. Blasting siding at full pressure strips paint, chews soft wood and forces water behind vinyl where it causes real damage. A soft wash flips the equation. We apply professional detergents at low pressure, let the chemistry break down the grime and organic growth, then rinse the whole exterior gently from top to bottom.

The result is the same clean without the risk. Vinyl, Hardie board, stucco and painted wood all keep their finish. Grime, green mildew, cobwebs and wasp nests come off the siding, soffits and trim. Most houses look newer from the street the same day.

Why Houses Here Need It

Gallatin Valley weather works on a house all year. Snow piles against the north side and feeds mildew in spring. Summer dust and pollen stick to everything. By the time the green film shows from the sidewalk, the siding has usually been carrying it for a couple of seasons. An occasional wash keeps the exterior healthy and keeps repaint day far away.

Common Questions

Will it hurt my paint or siding?

No. That is the whole point of soft washing. The detergents do the work and the rinse pressure is lower than a garden hose nozzle. It is safe for vinyl, Hardie board, stucco and painted wood.

What about my windows and screens?

They get rinsed along with everything else and usually look much better afterward. It is not a glass-polishing service, but grime and cobwebs around the frames come off.

How often should a house be washed?

Most homes here do well with a wash every year or two. North-facing walls and shaded sides may want it more often. We will tell you what we see when we quote it.
